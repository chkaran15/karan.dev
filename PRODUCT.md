# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Primary visitors are recruiters, employers, potential clients, collaborators, and agencies evaluating Karan Chaudhary as a software engineer and frontend developer. They arrive with limited time and need to quickly understand his level of craft, technical capability, taste, and reliability.

## Product Purpose

This portfolio positions Karan as a premium software engineer and frontend developer who builds animated, responsive, high-quality digital products. Success means visitors can immediately sense technical depth and design maturity, find credible evidence through services, work, experience, and case-study surfaces, and feel confident starting a hiring, client, or collaboration conversation.

## Brand Personality

Premium, technical, creative. The voice should feel precise and confident, with enough visual storytelling and motion to signal craft without becoming decorative, noisy, or difficult to use.

## Anti-references

This should not look like a generic SaaS template, a basic developer portfolio, a childish or overly colorful website, a cluttered agency site, a plain resume page, or a copied version of laugon.com or any reference site. Avoid default template section grammar, generic card grids, repeated tiny uppercase eyebrows, and overly literal developer aesthetics.

## Design Principles

- Lead with craft, then prove it with clarity. The first impression should feel premium, but the content still needs to answer why Karan is credible.
- Make motion purposeful. Animation should guide attention and express polish, never block reading, navigation, or interaction.
- Keep technical confidence human. The site should show engineering depth without collapsing into a cold SaaS dashboard or resume page.
- Treat theme support as brand support. Dark remains the default visual direction, while light mode must feel equally intentional and premium.
- Build reusable foundations. New About, Work, Services, Contact, and Case Study pages should use shared tokens, typography, motion rules, and component patterns.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Keep navigation keyboard accessible. Use semantic HTML. Keep text readable in both dark and light themes. Avoid motion that blocks usability or hides content from users, assistive technology, or reduced-motion environments.
